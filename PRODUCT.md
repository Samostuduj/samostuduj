# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

People in Czechia and Slovakia weighing whether to study a university-level
field **without** enrolling in a formal institution — prospective students,
current students questioning the traditional path, career-changers, and
self-directed learners. They arrive skeptical and a little intimidated: the
prevailing cultural assumption is that a degree is non-negotiable. Their context
is exploratory reading (often on mobile, often late at night), not task-driven
app use. The job to be done: *decide that self-study is legitimate and
achievable for them, and find a concrete path to start.*

## Product Purpose

`samostuduj.cz` makes the case that rigorous, flexible, self-directed study can
match or exceed formal higher education — and then hands the reader the tools to
do it: field guides (*obory*), structured study plans (*studijní plány*), a
manifesto, and a blog. It exists to close a gap: initiatives for self-directed
learning exist at the primary/secondary level but not at the university level.
Success is a visitor leaving convinced it's possible *and* knowing their first
step. Content is CC-BY-SA; the site is a public good, not a funnel.

## Brand Personality

Honest, grounded, quietly confident — never hype-y or salesy. Three words:
**capable, independent, unpretentious.** The voice practices what it preaches:
it teaches by showing, trusts the reader's intelligence, and never talks down.
The public-facing voice is **soft and welcoming** (invitation, not lecture);
the **Manifest** is where the harder, more opinionated stances live. The visual
identity is deliberately hand-made and text-forward — a monospace typeface
(Source Code Pro), indigo pen ink, and paper texture carried from hand-drawn
print collateral. It should feel like a thoughtful independent project by
people who actually study this way, not an institution or a startup.

Primary emotional target: the visitor feels **capable and empowered** —
"I could actually do this myself."

## Anti-references

- **Corporate university sites** — stock photos of smiling students,
  institutional blue, prestige signaling, bloated navigation.
- **Slick SaaS startups** — gradient-mesh heroes, floating dashboard mockups,
  "Get started free" CTAs everywhere, hype marketing voice.
- **MOOC platforms** (Coursera/Udemy) — course-card walls, ratings, upsells,
  cluttered catalog UI.
- **Generic AI-generated sites** — cream/sand body backgrounds, uppercase
  tracked eyebrows above every section, identical icon-card grids, gradient
  text. (The current build already avoids these; keep it that way.)

## Design Principles

- **Practice what you preach.** A site arguing for self-directed rigor must
  itself be clear, honest, and well-made. Craft is the argument.
- **Show, don't sell.** Demonstrate the value with real content (guides, plans,
  manifesto). No hype, no urgency tactics, no dark patterns.
- **Trust the reader.** Text-forward, calm, uncluttered. Respect intelligence
  and attention; earn the scroll rather than manufacturing engagement.
- **Soft voice, firm spine.** The public surface invites; the Manifest is where
  conviction is stated plainly. Keep those registers distinct.
- **Honest states.** When something isn't ready, say so plainly ("Připravujeme")
  rather than faking completeness — integrity over polish theater.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG AA**: body text contrast ≥4.5:1, large text ≥3:1, full keyboard
navigation, visible focus states, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for
every animation. Light and dark themes both meet these thresholds (current
accent pairs were contrast-checked). Czech-language content: ensure diacritics
render correctly and line-breaking/hyphenation stays clean at every breakpoint.
